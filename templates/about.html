    • Understand Brain Tumors

    Learn about the different types of brain tumors, how they are diagnosed and treated, and how they can affect your life.

    About Brain Tumor

    A brain tumor is an abnormal growth in the brain, classified as primary (originating in the brain) or secondary (spreading from other body parts). Symptoms can vary widely based on the tumor's size and location.

    Types of Brain Tumors

    Astrocytoma

    A tumor that originates from astrocytes, a type of glial cell.

    Meningioma

    A tumor that arises from the meninges, the protective membranes covering the brain.

    Glioblastoma

    A highly aggressive brain tumor that can arise from any glial cells.

    Oligodendroglioma

    A tumor that originates from oligodendrocytes, cells that produce the myelin sheath.

    Symptoms of brain tumors

    Headaches

    Persistent headaches that may get worse over time

    Seizures

    Sudden, uncontrolled movements or loss of consciousness

    Nausea and vomiting

    Feeling sick or being sick (vomiting)

    Changes in vision

    Blurred, double or loss of vision

    Diagnosis and Treatment

    Brain tumors are diagnosed through imaging tests like MRI and CT scans, followed by a biopsy to confirm the type. Treatment options vary based on the tumor type and location, and may include surgery, radiation therapy, and chemotherapy.

    Diagnosis

    Imaging tests (MRI, CT scans)

    Surgery

    Removing the tumor

    Radiation Therapy

    Targeted treatment to kill tumor cells

    Chemotherapy

    Using drugs to eliminate cancer cells

    Research and Statistics

    Current Research

    Researchers are exploring new treatments and therapies to improve outcomes for brain tumor patients.

    Statistics

    Brain tumors account for approximately 1.4% of all cancers, with an estimated 24,000 new cases diagnosed each year in the U.S.
